# Phase 4.2 process-supervision contract

## Status and scope

**PLANNED.** This is the future contract between the execution orchestrator and the confined validator process. It defines obligations, not protocol code or a final confinement technology. It implements the lifecycle described in [the architecture](phase42-confinement-architecture.md) and is constrained by [the requirements](phase42-confinement-requirements.md).

## Launch contract

The orchestrator supplies: a correlation/attempt identifier; approved validator artifact and dependency provenance references; immutable validation input; a bounded launch-input manifest covering structured input, staged artifacts, dependency manifests, configuration, input files, transport frames, compressed and expanded totals, count/depth/path constraints, and aggregate bytes; platform profile request; resource-budget profile reference; result schema/version; and requested audit classification. The supervisor rejects mutable ambient configuration and unaccounted input.

The supervisor shall validate all launch input against BUD-INPUT before process creation and create an immutable configuration record containing the selected platform profile, enforcement capability checks, workspace identifier, allowlisted environment names, deadlines, budget profile, expected executable identity and immutable-byte binding, expected authoritative process-control identity, ownership and restart-reconciliation identity, exact inherited-handle set, exact approved attempt-bound input/output/result/audit transport identities, monotonic privilege profile, dump/diagnostic policy, staging/input-accounting record, and result-transport limits. The validator working directory is a supervisor-owned staging workspace; host paths are not implicit inputs. Environment construction begins from empty or a reviewed minimal base and adds only allowlisted, non-secret variables. Input and output transport choices are **OPEN** under P42-D012, and the input-bound enforcement point is **OPEN** under P42-D018; both must be bounded and attributable to the attempt.

The supervisor shall validate archive/artifact structure before or during bounded extraction into its owned staging workspace. It shall normalize and contain paths; reject traversal, absolute, duplicate or case-colliding paths where applicable, symlinks, hardlinks, sparse files, device/special files, named pipes, sockets, recursive archives, and unsupported entries; and enforce compressed, expanded, per-file, aggregate, count, depth, path-length, metadata, and decompression-ratio bounds. On any crossed limit or ambiguity it stops extraction, removes partial staging, records deterministic malformed-input or input-limit evidence, and does not start the validator. The policy is **OPEN** under P42-D020.

Before launch, the supervisor shall bind the verified executable, runtime, source, dependency, configuration, and staged-artifact identity to the exact immutable bytes exposed to the validator. A filesystem path, mutable image tag, or mutable mount alone is not evidence of identity. The chosen candidate method is **OPEN** under P42-D014 and must reject substitution after verification, including path, symlink, hardlink, rename, dependency, interpreter, configuration, image, mount, or staged-file mutation.

The launch primitive shall establish the exact inherited-handle set required by CONF-HANDLE-001: approved descriptors, handles, sockets, pipes, IPC channels, process-control objects, and transport handles may cross only when listed; all other parent, supervisor, logging, audit, and platform-specific inheritable objects are closed, non-inheritable, or excluded. The supervisor refuses launch if that set cannot be established or verified. The policy choice is **OPEN** under P42-D017.

The local-IPC boundary required by CONF-IPC-001 shall expose only the exact approved supervisor-owned input, output, result, and audit transports. Each transport name and identity is unique to the attempt and bound to the authoritative process generation; all other local IPC, host-process, session, cross-attempt, debugger, inspector, and process-control channels are denied. Ordinary IP network denial does not satisfy this local-IPC requirement. The supervisor refuses any profile that cannot establish this boundary. P42-D021 is **OPEN**; no local-IPC mechanism is selected.

Before validator execution, the supervisor shall establish CONF-IDENTITY-001's minimum authority and monotonic non-escalation policy for the validator and descendants. It shall remove ambient credentials, supplementary groups, tokens, capabilities, and elevated-execution paths according to the approved platform profile, and refuse a profile that cannot prove no post-launch escalation. P42-D005 is **OPEN**; no privilege mechanism is selected.

Before validator execution, the supervisor shall disable dumps, diagnostic reports, inspector output, external crash reporting, and crash handlers by default, or establish the approved bounded attempt-workspace profile required by CONF-DUMP-001 and BUD-DUMP. It shall refuse a profile that cannot disable or confine prohibited artifacts and handlers. P42-D022 is **OPEN**; no dump-control mechanism is selected.

The supervisor shall bind the attempt to an authoritative generation-safe process or process-tree control identity before accepting lifecycle evidence. A numeric PID alone is not sufficient evidence of exact process identity. For any profile claimed supported, this identity is mandatory and is a kernel-backed process handle, pidfd, job object, cgroup/service identity, VM instance identity, or reviewed equivalent; human-readable PID data is supplemental. A profile that cannot establish it refuses launch. P42-D010 remains **OPEN** for the selected mechanism.

Before the validator can execute, the supervisor shall establish the reviewed ownership mechanism required by CONF-SUPERVISOR-001 and record its exact owner, process-generation, and attempt identity. The mechanism must ensure parent-death behavior or equivalent containment, preserve an accountable owner through a launcher/supervisor/session failure, or support generation-safe restart reconciliation. P42-D019 is **OPEN**; no ownership mechanism is selected.

stdout and stderr are diagnostic streams only: they are byte-counted, redacted as needed, and never accepted as structured success. Startup, validation, and shutdown deadlines are separate monotonic timers. Their values are **OPEN** until resource-budget review.

## Lifecycle and terminal handling

| Condition | Supervisor action | Required terminal category |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Startup succeeds | Record PID data, authoritative control identity, and exact inherited-handle evidence; begin validation deadline. | RUNNING (internal only) |
| Normal validator exit with valid result | Verify success proof, terminate/verify tree, clean workspace, emit audit event. | SUCCESS or VALIDATION_FAILURE |
| Normal exit without valid result | Stop transports, clean workspace, retain bounded diagnostics. | MALFORMED_OUTPUT or MISSING_RESULT |
| Startup deadline | Stop launch, terminate any started process tree, clean workspace. | STARTUP_TIMEOUT |
| Validation deadline | Initiate grace-period shutdown then force tree termination if needed. | VALIDATION_TIMEOUT |
| Shutdown deadline | Force remaining descendants and verify residual state. | SHUTDOWN_TIMEOUT |
| Signal or crash | Record observed signal/exit status, contain tree, clean workspace. | PROCESS_CRASH or SIGNALLED |
| Resource or policy breach | Stop acceptance, contain tree, clean workspace. | RESOURCE_LIMIT or POLICY_BREACH |
| Unauthorized local IPC, cross-attempt, or host-process interaction | Stop result transport, contain exact tree, and preserve bounded redacted evidence. | IPC_BOUNDARY_BREACH |
| Identity or privilege-policy breach | Stop result transport, contain exact tree, and preserve bounded redacted evidence. | IDENTITY_POLICY_BREACH |
| Unauthorized dump, diagnostic artifact, or crash handler | Stop result transport, contain tree/handler, and clean or quarantine artifact. | DUMP_POLICY_BREACH |
| Supervisor, launcher, parent, controlling-session, or host-cancellation loss; partially completed launch | Invalidate attempt; owner terminates or quarantines exact tree; stop result transport and retain bounded redacted evidence. | SUPERVISION_LOSS |
| Lost IPC or result transport | Prevent result acceptance; contain tree and clean or reconcile owned transports. | TRANSPORT_LOSS |
| Service restart with preexisting state | Reconcile only exact owned processes, workspaces, transports, and policy objects; invalidate affected attempts and contain unresolved state. | SUPERVISION_LOSS or RECONCILIATION_FAILURE |
| Cleanup verification failure | Escalate within ownership and prevent success. | CLEANUP_FAILURE |

Exit-code interpretation is **OPEN** under P42-D009, but codes must be stable, versioned, and never override supervisor observations. Signals, timeouts, malformed output, and oversized output are supervisor-owned failures, not validator-declared success. An oversized diagnostic stream or structured result invokes CONF-OUTPUT-001 or CONF-OUTPUT-002, respectively, from [the confinement requirements](phase42-confinement-requirements.md).

Child-process handling must enforce CONF-CHILD-001 and CONF-SUPERVISOR-001 from [the confinement requirements](phase42-confinement-requirements.md): each descendant is prevented or attributed to an owned process tree that the active owner can terminate even if the immediate supervisor fails. Process-tree termination first uses the reviewed graceful action, then forced termination after the configured grace period, followed by generation-safe residual-process and orphan-tree verification.

## Success proof, cleanup, retries, and audit

Before accepting success, the supervisor must prove all of the following: launch-input and safe-staging validation completed before execution; launch controls and exact inherited-handle policy were active; only the approved attempt-bound IPC channels existed and no cross-attempt or unrelated-process interaction occurred; the observed authoritative process identity matches the recorded generation rather than only a PID; minimum authority remained monotonic; a live accountable owner remained in force; verified provenance is atomically bound to the exact bytes executed or exposed; no unauthorized dump, crash report, diagnostic artifact, or crash handler was created; validation completed before its deadline; no supervision, policy, resource, local-IPC, privilege, dump, or transport breach occurred; exactly one bounded schema-valid result is correlated to the request; exit semantics match the result; mandatory assurance evidence of controls, identity, ownership, local IPC, privilege, dump policy, terminal outcome, and cleanup was recorded; the process tree is terminated; and cleanup or restart reconciliation verification succeeded. Any unknown, missing, or contradictory proof fails closed.

Cleanup covers pipes/IPC and their attempt-bound identifiers, process handles, temporary storage, staged inputs, output capture, ownership/policy handles, dumps/diagnostic artifacts/crash handlers, and process-tree state. It applies after success, validation failure, timeout, crash, forced termination, launch failure, supervision loss, local-IPC or privilege breach, dump-policy breach, or restart reconciliation. If a replacement owner restarts, it reconciles only state bound to the exact attempt and process generation, quarantines unresolved state, and never kills or adopts a replacement based only on PID. Retries are disabled by default; any future retry must create a fresh workspace and correlation identity, must not reuse an ambiguous result, and is bounded by [the resource budget](phase42-resource-budget.md).

Mandatory assurance evidence shall include correlation ID, timestamps, platform/capability profile, provenance identifiers and exact-byte binding, chosen policy profile, PID data plus authoritative control identity where supported, owner and restart-reconciliation identity, inherited-handle verification, approved transport identities and IPC-boundary observations, launch identity/privilege census and non-escalation observations, dump/diagnostic policy and artifact census, staging/input accounting, terminal category, observed exit/signal, byte/resource counters, and cleanup or reconciliation outcome. Optional diagnostic or operational logs may be best-effort, but their failure must not be confused with missing mandatory security evidence. Event content must be redacted so it does not disclose environment secrets or unbounded validator data. **PROHIBITED CLAIM:** a validator exit code or numeric PID alone proves successful confinement, exact process identity, ownership continuity, local-IPC isolation, or monotonic privilege.
