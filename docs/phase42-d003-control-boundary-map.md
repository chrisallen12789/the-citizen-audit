## Enforcement, observation, prevention, and detection

Enforcement owners prevent or terminate prohibited behavior using authority the validator does not possess. Observation owners collect independent state and events to demonstrate what the enforcement owner did. An observed limit without an enforcing limit does not satisfy a requirement, and a configured policy without evidence of effective attachment does not establish enforcement.

Prevention should stop unsafe launch, access, communication, privilege transition, substitution, or persistence. Detection is still required to prove denial, identify policy breaches, trigger termination, reconcile after loss, and prevent false success. Cleanup and residual scans detect and remediate remaining state; they do not convert an earlier prevention failure into an acceptable attempt.

The result gate is a control rather than a reporting convenience. It must reject success after any mandatory capability, policy, provenance, ownership, transport, termination, cleanup, or evidence failure even if the validator reports success.

## Boundary interactions

1. The orchestrator identifies an authorized request; the supervisor assigns the exact attempt identity.
2. The supervisor validates the exact deployment profile, enforcement capabilities, aggregate capacity and host reserve, and the complete device and mounted pseudo-filesystem inventory before creating validator-visible state.
3. The supervisor atomically reserves aggregate capacity before launch; requests exceeding the configured concurrent maximum or any aggregate resource or control-plane reserve are deterministically refused.
4. The artifact store and staging controller bind approved immutable bytes to a private read-only artifact view and bounded writable workspace.
5. The durable ownership layer creates the authoritative process and resource generation before the launcher starts untrusted code.
6. The launcher applies minimum identity, monotonic non-escalation, handle allowlisting, the minimal device and kernel-interface view, namespace or equivalent boundaries, network and local IPC isolation, syscall restriction, mandatory-access-control policy, dump policy, and exact transports before execution.
7. The kernel enforces; the supervisor and ownership layer observe. The validator can neither assert nor relax the effective policy.
8. The result transport and audit sink remain outside validator authority. Diagnostics remain distinct from the structured result and mandatory assurance record.
9. Every terminal outcome invokes complete-tree termination, cleanup, residual mount and descriptor scans, and audit finalization. The aggregate reservation is released only after verified cleanup or generation-safe reconciliation; failed cleanup retains or quarantines it, and supervisor or launcher loss requires reconstruction before new admission.

## Host-administrator and deployment boundary

The proposed composition trusts the administrator-controlled host, boot, kernel, service, policy, identity, artifact, audit, patch, and deployment configuration. Resistance to a malicious administrator, compromised kernel or hypervisor, firmware compromise, or physical access remains **OUT OF SCOPE** under P42-D002.

That trust does not permit unrecorded drift. A future supported profile must capture exact distribution, release, architecture, kernel, service manager, runtime, confinement policy, package, image, deployment, and capability identities. Any mismatch or inability to reproduce a mandatory control makes the profile unsupported and must fail closed. Evidence from a development container, desktop VM, WSL environment, CI label, or another Linux distribution cannot be transferred to production by analogy.

## Boundary nonclaims

- A container alone is not the security boundary; container packaging is not confinement evidence.
- Rootless execution alone, namespaces alone, seccomp alone, a low-privilege account alone, or AppArmor alone is not the security boundary.
- A VM is not automatically required and is not automatically sufficient; host, guest, transport, lifecycle, evidence, and cleanup controls remain necessary.
- Numeric PID logging is not authoritative generation-safe process identity.
- A verified pathname is not exact executed-byte identity.
- Ordinary network denial is not local IPC or cross-attempt isolation.
- Initial least privilege is not proof of post-launch non-escalation.
- Observation and logging are not substitutes for prevention and kernel enforcement.
- No control in this map is approved, implemented, tested, certified, deployed, or production-ready.

Phase 4.2 remains **PLANNED**. Implementation remains prohibited while Phase 4.1 is rejected.
