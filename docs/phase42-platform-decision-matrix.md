# Phase 4.2 platform decision matrix

## Decision posture

**OPEN:** no final implementation technology has been selected. Every mechanism below is **CANDIDATE**. Selection must satisfy [the requirements](phase42-confinement-requirements.md), have evidence prescribed by [the test plan](phase42-test-and-evidence-plan.md), and close the applicable entries in [the decision register](phase42-open-decisions-and-handoff.md). Familiarity or common use is not a selection rationale.

| Candidate mechanism | Isolation strength | Filesystem / IP-network / local-IPC control | Resource / process-tree control | Termination reliability | Operational complexity / portability | Observability / reproducibility | Dependency burden / deployment assumptions | Known gaps | Evidence before selection |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Direct child process with OS primitives | Limited unless combined with policies. | Platform-dependent path and network controls. | rlimits or equivalents may be partial. | Parent-child only unless tree control and parent-loss ownership are added. | Lower complexity; semantics differ by OS. | PID logging is supplemental; authoritative generation-safe process-control identity remains required for observation and reproducibility. | Native launcher permissions and supported OS APIs. | Does not alone deny all authority or guarantee orphan containment. | Negative boundary, supervisor-loss, and restart-reconciliation tests on each supported OS. |

## Selection criteria and nonclaims

A selected combination must map every applicable requirement to one or more enforcement points, declare gaps, and demonstrate fail-closed behavior when a control is unavailable. **REQUIRED:** platform identity and deployment assumptions must be evidence artifacts, not undocumented operator knowledge. CONF-SUPERVISOR-001 requires an explicit owner that remains effective across immediate-supervisor loss or performs generation-safe restart reconciliation; P42-D019 remains **OPEN**. P42-D020 remains **OPEN** for the bounded staging/extraction policy that must preserve exact-byte provenance and path safety. CONF-IPC-001 requires a separate local-IPC and cross-attempt policy; ordinary IP network denial does not supply this control, and P42-D021 remains **OPEN**. CONF-IDENTITY-001 requires non-escalation after launch, not only a low-privilege starting account; P42-D005 remains **OPEN**. CONF-DUMP-001 requires a reviewed dump/diagnostic policy and P42-D022 remains **OPEN**. A platform cannot be claimed supported without applicable supervisor-loss, orphan-containment, restart-reconciliation, exact-byte provenance, staged-artifact expansion/path-safety, local-IPC/cross-attempt isolation, privilege non-escalation, and crash-diagnostic evidence.

**PROHIBITED CLAIM:** containers alone prove security; ordinary IP network denial covers local IPC or host-process authority; a low-privilege launch identity alone prevents later escalation; any one mechanism provides complete isolation; or a development-platform observation certifies production behavior.
