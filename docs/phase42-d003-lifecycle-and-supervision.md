## Decision posture

**P42-D003: OPEN — RECOMMENDED lifecycle documented; pending independent review and explicit project-owner approval.** This document defines a future supervision lifecycle for the layered, OS-native Linux confinement composition proposed by P42-D003. It is architecture guidance, not a launcher protocol or an implementation specification.

[P42-D002](phase42-d002-threat-model.md) is **APPROVED** and supplies the hostile-validator and possible arbitrary-native-code attacker model. [P42-D001](phase42-d001-production-platform-baseline.md) remains **OPEN** and provisional; therefore every stage below is distribution-neutral and must be reproduced for each candidate production profile. Both coverage gaps defined in [the traceability record](phase42-d003-requirement-traceability.md#approved-threat-model-coverage-gaps) are already resolved only by their approved immutable traceability records. Final P42-D003 owner approval remains blocked until P42-D001 is approved, the recommendation is reconciled against that exact platform, and the reconciled package receives independent review and explicit P42-D003 owner action. Phase 4.2 remains **PLANNED**, and implementation remains prohibited while Phase 4.1 is rejected.

The lifecycle's approved gap-resolution records are [D003-TRACE-AGG-001](phase42-d003-gap-aggregate-governance.md) and [D003-TRACE-DEVICE-001](phase42-d003-gap-device-kernel-boundary.md). Their corresponding gaps are resolved only by those immutable approvals; reservation release still requires verified cleanup, and unsupported device or aggregate profiles still refuse before untrusted launch.

## Lifecycle invariants

- The external supervisor or reviewed durable owner is outside the validator trust boundary and remains authoritative for control activation, deadlines, termination, result acceptance, cleanup, and assurance evidence.
- A numeric PID is diagnostic data only. Control and evidence bind to the attempt identifier, exact process-generation identity, artifact identity, policy-object identities, workspace identity, and transport identities.
- Enforcement and observation are distinct. A counter, log entry, or capability report does not substitute for a kernel-enforced control and its tested failure response.
- All required controls and mandatory evidence hooks become active before validator execution. Unknown, unavailable, advisory-only, or irreconcilable mandatory capabilities cause refusal of the platform profile.
- Aggregate admission is a supervisor-owned control distinct from per-attempt budgets. Before any untrusted launch, the supervisor reserves capacity against the maximum admitted concurrent-attempt profile and the host reserve; individually valid attempts must not be admitted if their sum would overcommit that profile.
- Aggregate reservations remain charged until terminal cleanup is verified or a replacement owner completes generation-safe reconciliation. Restart must reconstruct reservation state before admitting new work, and ambiguous or failed cleanup retains or quarantines the charge.
- Network denial and local IPC isolation are separate controls. Success requires both where the approved profile requires them.
- Device and kernel-interface exposure is deny-by-default. The exact platform profile must inventory and govern device nodes, inherited device descriptors, pseudo-filesystems, mounted control filesystems, and kernel-control paths before launch; any required denial that cannot be established and observed makes the profile unsupported.
- Launch-time least privilege and post-launch non-escalation are separate controls. Both apply to the validator and every descendant.
- Verified paths are not proof of executed-byte identity. Staged objects must be immutable and bound to the exact bytes later executed or exposed.
- Every terminal path converges on owned-tree termination, generation-safe cleanup or reconciliation, residual scans, and audit finalization before success can be accepted.
- Optional diagnostics never replace mandatory redacted assurance evidence. Any diagnostic artifact remains sensitive, bounded, attempt-owned, and subject to the approved retention policy.

## Ordered future lifecycle

The stages are intentionally ordered. An implementation may combine atomic operations only if evidence still proves the ordering, prerequisite satisfaction, exact ownership, and fail-closed behavior of every stage.

| Stage | Lifecycle action | Enforcement and ownership | Mandatory observation and evidence | Fail-closed transition |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Profile selection | The orchestrator requests an approved platform profile by immutable profile identity; the external supervisor remains free to refuse it. | Attempt identifier, requested profile identity, platform claim, and caller authorization. | Reject an unknown, unapproved, ambiguous, or mismatched profile without exposing validator execution. |
| 2 | Capability discovery | A trusted probe outside the validator enumerates actual host, kernel, service-owner, identity, isolation, resource, dump-control, and cleanup capabilities. | OS and kernel identity, probe version, support state, and raw capability results bound to the attempt. | Treat probe error, contradictory state, or an unidentifiable host profile as unsupported. |
| 3 | Fail-closed capability validation | The supervisor compares discovered capabilities with the complete mandatory profile; observation-only support is not treated as enforcement. | Requirement-to-capability decision record, enforcement mode, policy versions, and deterministic refusal category. | Refuse launch if any mandatory control or evidence hook is unavailable, unknown, advisory when hard enforcement is required, or not independently supported for this profile. |
| 4 | Artifact identity verification | A trusted preflight component verifies the executable, runtime, validator source, dependencies, configuration, and input manifest as exact bytes. | Cryptographic digests or reviewed immutable identities, manifest validation, signer or provenance data where required, and verifier identity. | Reject missing, mutable, malformed, oversized, or ambiguous artifacts; a path or mutable tag is insufficient. |
| 5 | Resource-policy creation | The reviewed owner creates attempt-bound CPU, time, memory, process, thread, descriptor, filesystem, temporary-storage, transport, input, retry, grace, and diagnostic budgets before staging or launch consumes governed capacity. | Policy-object identities, approved values, enforcement modes, measurement sources, and creation timestamps. | Refuse missing, stale, shared, unenforceable, or incorrectly scoped mandatory budgets. |
| 6 | Aggregate reservation and admission | Before staging, extraction, or untrusted launch, the supervisor atomically reserves one admitted-attempt slot and the attempt's contribution to aggregate CPU, memory, process and thread count, descriptors and handles, filesystem and temporary storage, input and extraction work, stdout, stderr, result, IPC, dump and diagnostic volume, supervisor queues, pending results, audit buffers, retry amplification, and cleanup backlog. The admission calculation preserves capacity for the supervisor, durable owner, audit sink, and recovery path. | Immutable aggregate-profile identity, configured maximum concurrent attempts, reservation-ledger generation, before-and-after totals, attempt binding, host-reserve calculation, queue and backlog counters, and deterministic admission decision. | Refuse at zero capacity, at N+1, or whenever the sum of individually valid reservations would exceed any aggregate ceiling or host reserve; do not stage input or create an untrusted process. This control is governed by approved [D003-TRACE-AGG-001](phase42-d003-gap-aggregate-governance.md); its exact platform profile, numeric limits, and implementation remain unresolved. |
| 6A | Immutable staging | Under the admitted reservation, the supervisor creates a fresh bounded workspace and stages only verified objects using containment, structural, and exact-byte checks; executable artifacts become read-only. | Staging manifest, compressed and expanded counters, normalized paths, object identities, permission state, reservation binding, and proof no validator ran during staging. | Stop at the first limit or structural breach, remove partial staging, invalidate the attempt, and do not release its reservation until partial-state cleanup is verified. |
| 7 | Process-tree ownership creation | Before launch, a kernel, service-manager, cgroup, job, VM, watchdog, or reviewed equivalent owner is created for the complete future tree. | Owner identity, generation-safe control handle, empty initial census, parent-loss behavior, and restart-reconciliation metadata. | Refuse launch if durable ownership, exact identity, complete-tree termination, or safe reconciliation cannot be proved. |
| 8 | Identity and privilege reduction | The launch path selects the minimum attempt identity, removes ambient credentials and supplementary authority, and establishes monotonic non-escalation for descendants. | UID and GID or equivalent token census, groups, capabilities, credentials, no-new-privileges or equivalent state, and helper restrictions. | Refuse launch if minimum authority or non-escalation cannot be established and verified. |
| 9 | Namespace or equivalent boundary creation | The trusted launcher creates the approved mount, PID, IPC, network, user, or equivalent isolated views in the order required by the platform profile. | Boundary-object identities, membership, mount view, namespace or equivalent capability state, and ownership binding. | Refuse unsupported, shared, incorrectly ordered, or incompletely applied boundaries. |
| 9A | Device and kernel-interface policy establishment | Before launch, the trusted launcher establishes the exact deny-by-default device and kernel-interface policy required by [D003-GAP-DEVICE-001](phase42-d003-requirement-traceability.md#d003-gap-device-001). Character and block devices, device-node creation, inherited device descriptors, `/dev`, `/proc`, `/sys`, cgroupfs, debugfs, tracefs, securityfs, applicable runtime or service control filesystems, and kernel-control interfaces reachable through syscalls, capabilities, descriptors, or mounts are absent unless an exact minimal exception is reviewed for this platform. Approved read-only views are immutable where possible; mutation and host-global control remain denied. | Complete mounted-pseudo-filesystem and exposed-device inventory, mount identities and flags, descriptor census, policy digest, namespace and syscall or capability state, immutable exception manifest, and negative capability results. | Refuse launch on an unlisted object, writable or remountable control view, inherited authority, bypass path, namespace re-exposure, or inability to establish and observe denial. |
| 10 | Inherited-handle allowlisting | The launcher makes every parent object non-inheritable by default and exposes only exact approved input, output, result, audit, execution, and control objects. | Parent launch configuration, expected handle set, child-side census where supported, and transport identities. | Refuse launch on an unverified or unexpected descriptor, handle, socket, pipe, IPC object, logging object, or process-control object. |
| 11 | Network and local-IPC isolation | The profile applies ordinary IP denial and, separately, blocks unapproved local IPC, host-process interaction, discovery, signaling, inspection, and cross-attempt communication. | Network-policy identity, route and interface state, IPC boundary identity, allowlisted channel identities, peer binding, and negative capability status. | Refuse launch if either required boundary is absent; never treat IP denial as proof of local-IPC isolation. |
| 12 | Syscall and MAC policy application | The trusted launcher or durable owner applies the approved syscall restriction and mandatory-access-control policy after required setup and before untrusted execution. | Policy digests, load and attachment results, subject labels, enforcement mode, and allowed transition record. | Refuse launch on missing, permissive, stale, misattached, or unverifiable mandatory policy. |
| 13 | Validator process launch | The exact verified executable and runtime are started inside the prepared ownership and confinement objects with no mutable-path substitution window. | Launch timestamp, exact executed-object identity, authoritative process generation, tree membership, policy attachment, and initial census. | Terminate partial state and invalidate the attempt if launch identity, membership, policy, or byte binding is ambiguous. |
| 14 | Validation execution | The supervisor independently enforces deadlines and budgets, observes policy events, accounts for descendants, and preserves approved transports without trusting validator cooperation. | Monotonic timeline, resource counters and enforcement events, process-tree census, transport counters, policy events, and bounded diagnostics. | On timeout, limit, policy, identity, ownership, or transport breach, stop success acceptance and begin deterministic termination. |
| 15 | Result and evidence collection | The supervisor accepts at most one bounded schema-valid result from the exact attempt-bound result channel and collects mandatory assurance evidence separately from diagnostics. | Result-channel identity, byte count, schema result, attempt and process-generation binding, evidence completeness record, and duplicate or stale checks. | Reject missing, malformed, duplicate, oversized, stale, cross-attempt, provenance-mismatched, or incompletely evidenced results. |
| 16 | Terminal decision | A trusted decision function combines launch state, validator outcome, policy events, evidence completeness, and cleanup obligations; validator output alone is never authoritative. | Deterministic terminal category, reason chain, relevant counters, result disposition, and decision-maker identity. | Withhold success for any breach, uncertainty, missing mandatory evidence, supervision loss, or pending cleanup verification. |
| 17 | Process-tree termination | The reviewed owner closes approved transports, requests bounded orderly termination if allowed, then force-terminates the exact owned tree at the monotonic grace deadline. | Signal and action timeline, owner-scoped tree census, grace expiry, exit statuses, and proof unrelated processes were not targeted. | Escalate within reviewed owner authority; never select a target solely by numeric PID or adopt a replacement process. |
| 18 | Workspace and policy cleanup | The supervisor or replacement owner removes or quarantines attempt-owned inputs, outputs, IPC names, policy objects, workspaces, diagnostic artifacts, device-view objects, pseudo-filesystem mounts, control-filesystem mounts, and inherited or opened device and kernel-interface descriptors according to retention policy. | Idempotent cleanup journal, object-generation checks, retained-artifact inventory, mount and device inventory diff, descriptor closure, byte counts, and removal outcomes. | Mark cleanup failure and prevent success; preserve only bounded redacted evidence needed for remediation, and retain or quarantine the aggregate reservation. |
| 19 | Residual-process and residual-artifact scan | The reviewed owner checks for descendants, transports, namespaces or equivalent objects, policy objects, workspaces, dumps, reports, handlers, mounts, device objects, pseudo-filesystem and control-filesystem views, open descriptors, and other attempt residue. | Final tree and object census, storage scan, mounted-pseudo-filesystem and device inventory, descriptor census, stale-name check, unrelated-state exclusion, and scan capability status. | Mark the outcome failed or indeterminate, perform owner-scoped remediation, prevent success, and retain or quarantine reservation state if the scan fails, is unavailable, or finds residue. |
| 20 | Audit finalization | The supervisor commits a bounded redacted assurance record only after terminal classification and cleanup verification; optional logs remain distinguishable. | Complete event sequence, correlation identities, profile and artifact identities, control results, terminal category, cleanup proof, redaction result, and retention disposition. | Emit a deterministic audit-finalization failure and prevent success if mandatory evidence is missing, unbound, unredacted, or cannot be durably recorded. |
| 20A | Aggregate reservation release | Only after verified terminal cleanup and residual reconciliation, the supervisor atomically releases the attempt's slot and aggregate charges. A restarted owner reconstructs the ledger from durable attempt and ownership records before releasing or admitting capacity. | Release event bound to the exact attempt and ledger generation, zero-residue proof, before-and-after aggregate totals, retained or quarantined charges, and reconstructed-ledger comparison after restart. | Do not release ambiguous, failed-cleanup, or unreconciled state; quarantine the charge and refuse work that would exceed the remaining admitted profile. |

## Outcome and recovery matrix

Every row describes a required future result, not evidence that the behavior exists today.

| Outcome or disruption | Required supervisory response | Terminal and evidence implications |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Normal success | Validate the sole result and complete terminal decision, termination, cleanup, residual scans, audit finalization, and aggregate-reservation release against the exact attempt. | Accept success only after all controls remained active, mandatory evidence is complete, the owned tree is gone, cleanup is verified, residual scans are clear, and the exact reservation is released. |
| Validator failure | Ignore self-asserted success, preserve bounded diagnostics, classify the exit, and terminate any descendants. | Record validator failure plus exact exit and policy context; cleanup failure takes precedence over success eligibility. |
| Malformed output | Bound and safely discard malformed diagnostics or result data and terminate if the approved policy requires it. | Classify malformed result or output; never parse stdout or stderr as trusted success. |
| Timeout | Use the supervisor's monotonic deadline, invalidate any late result, and terminate the owned tree with bounded grace. | Record deadline, observed time, escalation actions, final census, cleanup, and residual scans. |
| Resource exhaustion | Apply the approved hard response at the attempt or tree boundary and prevent post-breach result acceptance. | Record the exact budget, counter source, limit event, host recovery evidence, and cleanup outcome. |
| Policy breach | Invalidate the attempt, close result acceptance, terminate the exact owned tree, and preserve bounded redacted assurance evidence. | Use a deterministic policy-breach category; no validator result can override it. |
| Supervisor loss | The durable ownership layer or reviewed replacement owner prevents unowned continuation, blocks success transport, and terminates or quarantines the tree. | The attempt is invalid; restart must reconcile exact generations before cleanup and final audit. |
| Launcher loss | Treat incomplete launch as untrusted state and let the precreated durable owner terminate or quarantine everything already attached. | Record the last completed stage and exact created objects; never resume the old launch transaction as success-capable. |
| Host cancellation | Bind cancellation to the exact attempt, close result acceptance, terminate the owned tree, and run full cleanup. | Record requester, monotonic cancellation time, actions, residual scans, and a cancelled terminal category. |
| Result-channel loss | Invalidate success, close remaining transports, and terminate the owned tree if it is still running. | Record transport identity and loss state; missing or disconnected result is not validator success. |
| Crash | Contain crash handlers and diagnostic artifacts, suppress or bound dumps under the approved profile, and terminate remaining descendants. | Record crash cause where safely observable, dump-policy state, handler census, artifact inventory, and cleanup. |
| Forced termination | Target only the authoritative owned generation and apply the reviewed escalation policy. | Record force action, target identity, exit confirmation, retained diagnostics, and residual scans. |
| Partial launch | Invalidate the attempt, attach created state to the precreated owner where applicable, terminate it, and remove staged or policy objects. | Record completed and failed stages; prove validator execution either did not begin or was terminated without result acceptance. |
| Restart reconciliation | A replacement owner inventories recorded attempts and reconstructs the aggregate reservation ledger before admitting new work, then reconciles process generations, ownership objects, transports, workspaces, policy state, mounted pseudo-filesystems, exposed devices, and open descriptors before accepting new terminal claims or releasing charges. | Unreconciled or ambiguous state fails closed and retains or quarantines its reservation; a restarted component cannot infer ownership from PID or name alone. |
| Orphan cleanup | Locate only state provably owned by the recorded attempt, terminate or quarantine it, and verify absence. | Record generation-safe ownership evidence and unrelated-state exclusions; ambiguity prevents destructive adoption and success. |
| Stale process identity | Reject the stale handle, result, evidence, or control request and reconcile the recorded attempt without acting on a replacement. | Record the identity mismatch and stale-control category; success remains impossible. |
| PID reuse | Compare the authoritative kernel, owner, service, cgroup, job, or VM identity rather than the reused numeric PID. | Prove the replacement was neither killed nor adopted and reject evidence attributed only by PID. |
| Partial staging | Stop extraction or copy-in immediately, remove all partial attempt-owned output, and never enter launch stages. | Record the crossed structural or size limit, cleanup proof, and execution-not-started evidence. |
| Dump or diagnostic artifact creation | Enforce the approved suppression or bounded confinement policy, terminate unauthorized handlers, and remove or quarantine sensitive artifacts. | Unauthorized location, handler, size, type, or retention is a policy breach; record artifact census and residual scan. |

## Restart and concurrency constraints

Restart records must carry enough immutable identity to distinguish a live attempt from stale files, reused names, replacement processes, and another concurrent attempt. Reconciliation is generation-safe and idempotent: it may terminate, remove, quarantine, or retain only state proven to belong to the recorded attempt and within the reviewed owner's authority. Ambiguity fails closed and must not trigger broad process killing or deletion.

Attempts use unique, non-reusable transport and workspace identities and cannot discover or signal one another. The durable ownership layer must survive or safely react to supervisor, launcher, orchestrator-connection, and session loss. New work must not conceal unresolved residual state from an older attempt.

The aggregate ledger must prevent overcommit across independently valid attempts and account for pending admission, active execution, results awaiting disposition, audit and retry amplification, and cleanup backlog. Zero capacity and N+1 admission fail deterministically before launch. Capacity allocated to supervision, ownership, audit, and recovery is not available to validators. Reservation release occurs only after verified cleanup or generation-safe reconciliation, never merely because a validator exited or a supervisor restarted.

Every restart and terminal path also reconciles the exact device and kernel-interface policy: created device nodes, device descriptors, `/dev`, `/proc`, `/sys`, cgroupfs, debugfs, tracefs, securityfs, runtime or service control filesystems, related mount or namespace objects, and applicable syscall- or capability-reachable control interfaces. The final inventory must match the approved minimal platform view and show that no attempt-owned mount, descriptor, device object, or authority remains.

## Nonclaims and unresolved decisions

This lifecycle constrains but does not resolve P42-D004 through P42-D022. Exact service ownership, cgroup policy, namespace arrangement, identity, syscall filtering, mandatory-access-control policy, artifact binding, transport schema, resource values, crash behavior, and retention rules remain **OPEN**. A container runtime, VM, service manager, namespace, syscall filter, mandatory-access-control policy, rootless account, or low-privilege account alone is not proof of the composition. JavaScript-level controls remain defense in depth and are not the Phase 4.2 security boundary.

**PROHIBITED CLAIM:** this recommended lifecycle shows that confinement is implemented, tested, accepted, certified, deployed, production-secure, or completely isolating.
