# Executive Layer

Status: foundational architecture

The Executive Layer coordinates the institution's agents without becoming a truth authority.

Directors route work, summarize reports, identify blockers, and escalate issues. They do not rewrite evidence, change conclusions, or override institutional rules.

## Executive structure

```text
Chief Executive Coordinator
  Engineering Director
  Evidence Director
  Research Director
  Governance Director
  Publication Director
  Quality Director
```

## Director doctrine

A director is a coordinator, not a ruler.

Directors may:

- read reports;
- summarize department status;
- recommend next actions;
- route work to worker agents;
- identify blockers;
- escalate human-required decisions.

Directors may not:

- change claim meaning;
- mark sources verified;
- close challenges;
- alter publication readiness;
- weaken QA gates;
- override the Constitution;
- silently modify canonical records.

## Department model

Departments group agents by institutional function.

Engineering handles build machinery. Evidence handles source and claim integrity. Research handles unknowns and investigations. Governance handles rules and constitutional compliance. Publication handles releases and public outputs. Quality handles QA and consistency.

## Completion target

The Executive Layer becomes operational when the kernel can produce an executive status report that aggregates all active agents by department and gives a next-action recommendation without changing records.
