# Operating Principles

Status: institutional doctrine  
Date: 2026-07-05

These principles govern The Citizen Audit, its software, its agents, and its human contributors.

## 1. Evidence before opinion

No assertion deserves institutional standing without evidence, citation, decision record, or explicit unknown status.

## 2. Transparency before trust

The public should be able to inspect how records were created, changed, challenged, preserved, and published.

## 3. Verification before persuasion

The institution exists to make verification easier, not to win arguments.

## 4. Unknowns are first-class records

A missing source, unresolved definition, inaccessible dataset, disputed method, or incomplete official record must be logged as an unknown rather than hidden in prose.

## 5. Authority is constrained

Founder, contributor, reviewer, agent, and software authority must be limited by public rules.

## 6. Automation is subordinate to governance

Agents may automate process. Agents may not silently override governance.

## 7. Truth-bearing changes require traceability

Changes to claims, sources, decisions, publication readiness, or institutional standards require auditable records.

## 8. Permanent identifiers are institutional memory

IDs must not be reused to conceal change. Withdrawal, replacement, correction, or supersession must preserve history.

## 9. Derived files are replaceable

Generated pages, indexes, manifests, APIs, and dashboards may be regenerated from canonical records. Canonical records require higher scrutiny.

## 10. The founder is not exempt

The founder must remain subject to the same evidentiary and procedural rules as every contributor and agent.

## 11. Public rules beat private intention

Good intention is not enough. The institution must be designed so good intention is reinforced by public rules and bad action leaves evidence.

## 12. Scale through narrow authority

The institution should scale by adding many constrained agents, not by creating a few unconstrained agents.

## 13. No silent history rewriting

Corrections are allowed. Revision without trace is not.

## 14. The website is only one output

The website is an application of the institution. The records, rules, and audit trails are the institution.

## 15. The software serves the institution

Technology decisions must serve long-term reproducibility, transparency, preservation, and public verification.
