# The Citizen Audit in 2035

Status: North Star doctrine  
Date: 2026-07-05

This document describes the institution The Citizen Audit is being built to become. It is not a marketing plan. It is a design target.

## Institutional identity

By 2035, The Citizen Audit is a permanent public auditing institution whose primary product is not opinion, commentary, or political persuasion. Its primary product is verifiable public evidence infrastructure.

The institution exists so ordinary citizens, journalists, researchers, public servants, and contributors can inspect public claims through records rather than belief.

## Scale target

The institution is capable of supporting:

- hundreds or thousands of public audits;
- many independent contributors;
- many specialized agents;
- reproducible evidence packages;
- public challenges and corrections;
- public APIs;
- permanent source-preservation records;
- institutional memory across years and leadership changes.

The institution may scale to very large numbers of agents, but those agents remain narrow, constrained, logged, and subordinate to public rules.

## Founder continuity

The institution must remain understandable and operable if the founder disappears, steps back, becomes unavailable, or is replaced.

The repository must contain enough doctrine, rules, records, procedures, and automation for a qualified successor to continue the institution without private founder memory.

## Public capability

A member of the public can:

- inspect every published audit;
- trace claims to sources;
- inspect source health;
- view unknowns;
- read decision logs;
- submit challenges;
- download publication packages;
- inspect machine-readable records;
- reproduce major conclusions from public data.

## Bias constraint

The institution does not claim to eliminate bias by trusting people or models. It reduces bias by limiting discretion through rules, audit trails, evidence requirements, competing review, and visible unknowns.

The founder, contributors, agents, and reviewers are all treated as possible sources of error.

## Governance target

The Constitution, Charter, Citizen Standard, Contributor Oath, Unknowns Register, decision logs, and kernel permission model govern the institution more than any single person.

Major institutional changes require visible records.

## Agent workforce

Agents are organized by departments, not chaos.

Departments include:

- Engineering;
- Evidence;
- Research;
- Archives;
- Quality;
- Governance;
- Publication;
- Public Challenge Intake;
- Institutional Architecture.

Most agents are small workers. Directors coordinate. The kernel enforces boundaries.

## Website role

The website is only one interface into the institution.

The institution also exists through:

- canonical records;
- public APIs;
- downloadable evidence packages;
- archive manifests;
- event logs;
- agent reports;
- institutional memory;
- release artifacts.

## Success condition

The institution succeeds if a skeptical stranger can inspect a public claim, trace it to evidence, see what remains unknown, challenge the record, and understand how the institution responded.

Trust comes with time. Verification starts immediately.
