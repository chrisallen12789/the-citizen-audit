Audit 002 Reader Guide

How to Read the VA Audit

Audit 002 is a traceable public-record review of selected Veterans Affairs funding, oversight, and outcome questions.

Open Audit 002FindingsLimitations

The question

The guiding question is: for every dollar Congress appropriates to VA, where does it go, who handles it, what does it buy, and what does the veteran receive?

The audit does not answer that entire question for every VA program. It publishes a validated evidence core with explicit boundaries.

The seven supported findings

2024 shortfall episodes

The VBA and VHA funding episodes had different structures and should not be collapsed into one simple narrative.

FY2026 budget structure

The record budget increase is mainly a mandatory-side event, with Toxic Exposures Fund effects central to the analysis.

Staffing measurement

The audit records a measurement-integrity issue because VHA staffing adequacy is measured by an instrument the agency publicly disputes.

Suicide-prevention reach

VA reporting creates a boundary around spending-to-outcome claims because many decedents were outside VHA care in the prior year.

EHR modernization

EHR modernization remains a dated finding that requires current-status checks.

Community care

Community-care oversight records show growth beside appointment, network, data, and oversight measurement concerns.

Financial statements

A clean audit opinion can coexist with named material weaknesses and significant deficiencies.

How to verify it

  1. Start with the supported finding.
  2. Open the related claim group.
  3. Check the evidence map.
  4. Check the source-gate page.
  5. Read the explicit limitations before repeating the finding.
