Decision Log

Methodology decisions belong in public, even when the full numbered register still needs canonical-asset extraction.

Decision-log status

The repository currently does not include the canonical PDF asset or an extracted numbered D-001 through D-026 decision register. This page therefore publishes only the binding methodology decisions that are already visible in converted web sections and preserves the official numbering by not inventing replacement IDs.

Web-extracted methodology decision

Gross, not net

The web edition preserves the audit rule that figures represent gross money directed toward non-U.S. recipients or non-citizens, not net fiscal impact after taxes.

Visible references: Methodology, Section 1, Section 2

Web-extracted methodology decision

No blended grand total

The platform keeps incompatible bases separate and does not synthesize one headline total from disbursements, obligations, cumulative military lanes, and federal-plus-state figures.

Visible references: Section 1, Section 3, Section 5, Section 14 placeholder

Web-extracted methodology decision

No population-share modeling

Where a federal program does not publish a citizenship-status spending breakout, the site records the gap instead of allocating dollars from population share.

Visible references: Methodology, Section 8, Section 9, Section 13 placeholder

Web-extracted methodology decision

Citizen-child exclusion

Benefits paid for U.S.-citizen children in mixed-status households remain excluded from non-citizen spending totals.

Visible references: Section 1, Methodology, Section 8

Web-extracted methodology decision

Recipient separated from economic beneficiary

The publication distinguishes the direct recipient of federal spending from the economic beneficiary that ultimately captures value, especially in provider-heavy or contractor-heavy lanes.

Visible references: Section 2, Section 5, Section 6, Section 7

Web-extracted methodology decision

Drawdown value separated from replacement cost

Military drawdown transfer values are not treated as the same thing as taxpayer replacement appropriations, preventing duplicate counting.

Visible references: Section 2, Section 5
