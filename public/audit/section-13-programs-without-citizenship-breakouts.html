13.K Key Findings

  • This is the audit’s central documented finding: for these programs the non-citizen-specific federal figure cannot be derived from primary evidence, and no estimate is fabricated.
  • The deliverable is a gap register identifying the program, agency, reason, and evidence that would resolve it. No figure is assigned; no gap is zeroed or summed.

13.2 Treatment

Total A contribution is a documented gap, not a number. Total B, where dollars do flow, is generally captured by U.S. providers such as hospitals, schools, clinics, and landlords. This register is the empirical backbone of the transparency thesis developed further in Appendix B and Section 15.

13.HR Hostile review

“Section 13 gaps are convenient omissions that let you cherry-pick.” This does not land. Each gap names the owner, agency, and exact data product that would resolve it. Gaps are never zeroed or summed. The gap is the finding. The section survives hostile review.

13.QA / LOCK

No figure is fabricated; every gap is typed and attributed; each is cross-referenced to Section 14 Table 14-G and Section 15. Section 13 is locked in Version 1.0.
