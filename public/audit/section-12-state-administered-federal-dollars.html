C-014

State-administered federal dollars add zero net-new dollars by design

Section 12 is a routing and reconciliation lens that guards against double counting once federal money passes through state systems.

Sources
S-062 S-065

12.K Key Findings

  • Section 12 is a non-additive reconciliation lens: every major state-administered federal dollar stream is already counted in its owning section, so Section 12 contributes $0 net-new to Total A.
  • Its value is as a double-count guard and as a map of where the federal-to-state-to-local chain loses citizenship detail.

12.2 Total A contribution

$0, because the section is non-additive. Its purpose is to prevent double-counting of Sections 6-11 dollars and to document that the state-administration layer is where much of the citizenship detail is lost.

12.HR Hostile review

“Non-additive sections were added and state-administered dollars were double-counted.” This does not land. Section 12 contributes $0 by construction under rule E5; the map records only where each stream is already counted. The section survives hostile review.

12.QA / LOCK

Non-additivity preserved at $0 net-new; state-only funds excluded; CCDF, SSBG, CSBG, and LIHEAP route to Section 13. Section 12 is locked in Version 1.0.
