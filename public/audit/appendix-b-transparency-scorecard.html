B.1 Rating categories

  1. Transparent - agency publishes status-level data permitting a primary or reproducible figure.
  2. Partially transparent - a published figure exists but is not resolved to the federal-only and or population-isolated line.
  3. Opaque by reporting choice - data exist internally but are not published by status.
  4. Opaque by technical limit - data systems cannot isolate the population.
  5. Uncollectable by law or design - status is not or may not be collected.

B.3 Net finding

Transparency is highest for the smallest identifiable program, SSI, and lowest for the largest potential exposures, the inverse of what a complete forensic accounting would require. No row asserts a hidden dollar amount; each “no” is a documented gap with a named resolving data product linked in Appendix A and Section 13.
