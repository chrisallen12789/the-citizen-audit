Civic Accountability Platform

Public records. Plain English. Challengeable findings.

The Citizen Audit is a nonpartisan research platform built to help ordinary citizens inspect government records, understand what the evidence actually shows, and separate documented findings from political noise.

The site is no longer organized around one audit. It is the permanent home for multiple audits, source trails, articles, corrections, and public review standards.

What This Is

The Citizen Audit is an institution, not a single report.

Pro-citizen, not pro-party.

The project does not exist to defend government or attack government. It exists to make public records readable, testable, and harder to hide behind slogans.

Every audit stands on its own.

Each audit gets its own claims, sources, decision log, open questions, corrections, and downloads. The main site remains neutral until a reader chooses a specific audit.

Fund hosting, archival work, FOIA costs, records preservation, and future investigations without buying influence over findings.

Operating Rule

Evidence is public. Uncertainty stays visible. Corrections are part of the system.

A reliable audit does not pretend every gap is solved. When the record runs out, the site says so. When a claim is wrong, the correction trail should show what changed and why.
