D-021 - Ownership map for domestic sections

Decision records preserve the publication's binding methodological rules in one addressable location.

Back to decision log Search related records Open explorer

Published rule

Domestic federal dollars are owned by one canonical section so routed programs are not counted twice. ORR, Medicaid, TANF-related pathways, and state-administered lenses keep their owning section explicit.

Decision ID: D-021

References: Section 6 Section 7 Section 8 Section 9 Section 10 Section 11 Section 12 Section 13

Why this page exists

This record makes the numbered methodology rule addressable in the web edition so sources, sections, and future traceability features can point back to the same canonical decision.

Claims governed

C-007 - Section 6

ORR is owned as a domestic lane, not an overseas add-on

Refugee and entrant assistance stays in one canonical domestic section so it is not double-counted against foreign-assistance accounts.

C-008 - Section 6

ORR provider capture remains a first-class unresolved limitation

The public record identifies the ORR lane but does not yet publish a defensible entrant-versus-provider split.

C-009 - Section 7

Emergency Medicaid is measurable on a federal-plus-state basis, not a federal-only basis

Section 7 preserves the measurable lane while carrying the missing federal-only share as an explicit open question.

C-010 - Section 8

SNAP noncitizen dollars are not backfilled with modeled estimates

Section 8 documents the eligibility framework and refuses unsupported benefit attribution by population share.

C-011 - Section 9

SSI is one of the clearest direct-cash domestic lanes

The current edition relies on SSA-published noncitizen recipient and payment inputs while preserving the point-in-time limitation.

C-012 - Section 10

Housing eligibility rules already exclude many ineligible uses through Section 214 and proration

Section 10 is strongest on program mechanics, not on a missing published eligible-noncitizen outlay total.

C-013 - Section 11

K-12 and student-aid analysis is constrained by legal collection limits and eligibility structure

Section 11 treats missing education spending ledgers as a real transparency limit rather than something to impute.

C-014 - Section 12

State-administered federal dollars add zero net-new dollars by design

Section 12 is a routing and reconciliation lens that guards against double counting once federal money passes through state systems.

C-015 - Section 13

The gap register is itself a finding, not an empty placeholder

Section 13 names programs where the record does not support a defensible citizenship breakout and refuses to fabricate missing values.
